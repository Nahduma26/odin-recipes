Drunken Noodles

Description

Drunken Noodles, also known as Pad Kee Mao, is a tantalizing and flavorful Thai dish that boasts a perfect balance of spicy, savory, and slightly sweet flavors. This popular street food is renowned for its vibrant taste and aromatic blend of ingredients. The dish typically features wide rice noodles stir-fried with an assortment of ingredients that contribute to its distinctive profile.

The star of the show is the flat rice noodles, which absorb the savory soy sauce and chili-infused flavors during the cooking process. These noodles are stir-fried to perfection, resulting in a delightful combination of chewy and slightly crispy textures. The dish often includes a generous medley of fresh vegetables such as bell peppers, onions, and Thai basil leaves, contributing to both the visual appeal and the overall taste.

What sets Drunken Noodles apart is the inclusion of spicy elements, usually provided by the addition of bird's eye chilies or chili paste. The heat is tempered by the umami-rich soy sauce and a hint of sweetness from ingredients like sugar or sweet soy sauce. The dish might also feature proteins like succulent slices of beef, chicken, shrimp, or tofu, enhancing its versatility and appeal to a wide range of taste preferences.

Drunken Noodles are a delightful experience for those who appreciate bold and dynamic flavors. The name itself, "Drunken Noodles," is said to originate from the notion that this dish is the perfect remedy for a hangover, making it an ideal choice for those seeking a comforting and flavorful Thai culinary adventure.

Ingredients

  • Shallots
  • Carrots
  • Garlic
  • Fresh Ginger
  • Zucchini
  • Bell Pepper
  • Green Onions
  • Roma Tomatoes
  • Thai Basil (Normal or Holy)
  • Brown Sugar
  • Oyster Sauce
  • Soy
  • Sesame Oil or Canola
  • Fish Sauce
  • Chicken or other protein
  • Onion
  • Wide Dried Rice Noodles
  • Water
  • Red Chili Sauce

Steps

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain, rinse with with water and set aside.
  2. Prepare the sauce: Mix sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
  3. Cook chicken and vegetables. Heat oil in wok or large skillet over high heat. Add shallots and carrot and cook for 2 minutes. Add more oil and cook chicken. Add garlic and ginger, add bell pepper, zucchini, and the whites of the chopped green onion and saut√©.
  4. Add noodles and sauce. Toss and cook for a few minutes until warmed through. Remove from heat and stir in chopped basil.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with remaining green onion and extra chili sauce, sriracha, or crushed red pepper, for added spice.
